Parenthetical remarks.
parenthetical /ˌpærənˈθetɪkl/
given as extra information in a speech or piece of writing

There are divergences between the legal systems of the two countries.
divergence /daɪˈvɜːrdʒəns/
the process or fact of separating or becoming different

An electricity worksheet's simply a piece of paper listing all of your household appliances with their wattage.
worksheets /ˈwɜːrkʃiːt/
a sheet that is used in making preliminary plans, auxiliary computations, notes, or comments as a guide in doing some piece of work

Always switch off appliances that aren't in use.
appliances /əˈplaɪəns/
a machine that is designed to do a particular thing in the home, such as preparing food, heating or cleaning

There were long queues at polling stations.
queue /kjuː/
a line of people, cars, etc. waiting for something or to do something

There's easy access to the centre of the city by tram.
tram /træm/
(US English streetcar, trolley) a vehicle driven by electricity, that runs on rails along the streets of a town and carries passengers

Each table had pots of butter and marmalade.
marmalade /ˈmɑːməleɪd/
jam made from oranges, lemons, etc., eaten on bread, especially at breakfast

Numbers are examples of quantifiers.
quantifiers /ˈkwɑːntɪfaɪər/
determiner or pronoun that expresses quantity, for example ‘all’ or ‘both’

The police began the manhunt for the escaped prisoner.
manhunt /ˈmænhʌnt/
​an organized search by a lot of people for a criminal or a prisoner who has escaped

Violence intensified during the night.
intensified /ɪnˈtensɪfaɪd/
(SYNONYM heighten) ​to increase in degree or strength; to make something increase in degree or strength

His right knee, which was operated on in 1983 and reinjured last month.
reinjured /reɪndʒər/
to injure (something or someone) again

Any chance of a drink? This weary traveller's about to collapse from thirst.
weary /ˈwɪri/
(SYNONYM exhausted)very tired, especially after you have been working hard or doing something for a long time

We climbed up the rickety wooden stairs which led to the third floor.
rickety /ˈrɪkəti/
not strong or well made; likely to break

The best way to get up the tree was by the rope ladder.
rope ladder /ˈrəʊp lædər/
a ladder made of two long ropes connected by short pieces of wood or metal with equal spaces between

The island's twenty degrees south of the equator.
equator /ɪˈkweɪtər/
an imaginary line around the earth at an equal distance from the North and South Poles

Mary stroked the cat's downy fur.
downy /ˈdaʊni/
​covered in something very soft, especially hair or feathers

He's a beautiful dog. Can I stroke him?
stroke /strəʊk/
to move your hand gently and slowly over an animal’s fur or hair

She suffered from dizziness and blurred vision.
blurred /blɜːrd/
​not clear; without a clear outline or shape

He's promised to reform.
reforming /rɪˈfɔːm/
to improve your behaviour; to make somebody do this

He looked tired and dishevelled.
dishevelled /dɪˈʃevld/
(SYNONYM unkempt) of hair, clothes or somebody’s general appearance) very untidy
