Each language has a different vowel system.
vowels /ˈvaʊəl/
a speech sound in which the mouth is open and the tongue is not touching the top of the mouth, the teeth, etc., so that the flow of air is not limited, for example /ɑː, e, ɔː/

The sounds of laughter and singing mingled in the evening air.
mingle /ˈmɪŋɡl/
(Synonyms mix) to combine or make one thing combine with another

The princess wasn't recognized and mingled freely with the crowds.
mingled /ˈmɪŋɡld/
(SYNONYM circulate) to move among people and talk to them, especially at a social event

These tasks were repetitive, lengthy, and lacking any intrinsic interest.
intrinsic /ɪnˈtrɪnzɪk/
(COMPARE extrinsic) belonging to or part of the real nature of something/somebody

She was motivated by the idea of extrinsic rewards such as salary rather than intrinsic ones such as pride and self-respect.
extrinsic /eksˈtrɪnzɪk/
(COMPARE intrinsic) ​not belonging naturally to somebody/something; coming from or existing outside somebody/something rather than within them

demonstrations /ˌdemənˈstreɪʃn/
a public meeting or a march (= an organized walk by many people) at which people show that they are protesting against or supporting somebody/something

Demonstrations were held as a gesture of solidarity with the hunger strikers.
solidarity /ˌsɑːlɪˈdærəti/
support by one person or group of people for another because they share feelings, opinions, aims, etc.

hold /həʊld/

vicious /ˈvɪʃəs/
